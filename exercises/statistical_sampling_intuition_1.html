Does a random sample taken from a population always represent it well?

Increase the sample size

What do you have to do to increase the likelihood that a sample you are going to take will be a "good, representative sample" of a population?
  • Decrease the population standard deviation
  • Increase the population size
  • Increase the sample size
  • All of the above
  • None of the above

Move the sliders to change the population parameters (mean, standard deviation, size), and then change the sample size (which is a subset of the population size), and observe the differences between the population and sample parameters (mean, standard deviation).

Decreasing the population standard deviation will increase the reliability (or true representation) of a taken sample (think of the extreme case where every member of the population is the same, that is, the standard deviation is 0. In that case, taking a sample of 1 will be enough).
But in reality, do you have control over the population parameters?

Think of what it means to set the sample size equal to the size of the entire population. While not practical in many cases (very difficult or costly), it helps to think of "cases in the extreme". Try it!

In the "extreme case" where the sample size equals the size of the entire population, the mean and standard deviation of both is identical. Therefore, in order to create a "good, representative sample", it is important to have as large a sample as practical, in addition to sampling randomly!
